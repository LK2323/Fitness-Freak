Details.

✅ **Signs of a beginner:**

- Bench press below 0.75x body weight.

- Squat below body weight.

- Struggles to perform more than 10 push-ups or bodyweight squats.

- Gets fatigued quickly in workouts.

✅ **Training Focus:**

- Learning proper form.

- Building consistency.

- Strengthening stabilizer muscles and tendons.

- Developing endurance and work capacity.

Details.

As a beginner in the gym, follow these essential steps to build a strong foundation:

1.Set Clear Goals

- Decide whether you want to gain muscle, lose fat, increase endurance, or improve overall fitness.

2.Learn Basic Exercises

- Strength Training: Squats, push-ups, lunges, deadlifts, bench press, and rows.

- Cardio: Treadmill walking, cycling, or jump rope for warm-ups and endurance.

3.Focus on Proper Form

- Avoid lifting heavy weights initially. Learn correct posture and technique to prevent injuries.

4.Follow a Balanced Routine

- Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of dynamic stretching or light cardio.

- Workout: A mix of strength and cardio exercises (3-5 times per week).

- Cool-down: Stretching to improve flexibility and recovery.

5.Start with Full-Body Workouts

- Train major muscle groups 3-4 times a week before splitting workouts into body parts.

6.Manage Rest & Recovery

- Take rest days to allow muscles to recover and grow.

- Get 7-9 hours of sleep for optimal recovery.

7.Maintain a Healthy Diet

- Eat protein-rich foods (chicken, eggs, fish, lentils).

- Consume complex carbs (rice, oats, whole grains) for energy.

- Stay hydrated by drinking enough water.

8.Track Progress

- Keep a workout log to monitor improvements in strength and endurance.

9.Avoid Overtraining

- Start slow and gradually increase intensity over weeks.

10.Stay Consistent & Patient

- Results take time; consistency is key to progress.
